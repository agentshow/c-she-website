SAT Methodology

SAT's learning methodology can be summarized in three words: "Learn for Use." This is not a slogan, but a set of concrete, executable learning principles and practical methods.

Core Principles

Problems First, Tools Second

First clarify the problem to solve, then find the suitable AI tool. It's not about learning tools first and then finding applications, but selecting tools in reverse based on needs.

Minimum Viable Learning

Don't pursue comprehensive mastery of AI tools, but learn the minimum knowledge set needed to solve the current problem. Quick start, quick validation, quick iteration.

Measurable Results

Every learning session must have clear effectiveness metrics -- how much has efficiency improved? How much has output increased? Learning without measurable results is a waste of energy.

Experience Exchange Network

The fundamental difference between SAT and traditional AI courses lies in: traditional courses involve one-way knowledge transfer (instructor to student), while SAT involves two-way experience exchange (mutual learning among members). Everyone's experience accumulated in practice becomes part of the knowledge base, and later learners can get started quickly based on predecessors' experience.

You don't need to step into the same pitfalls others have already fallen into.

The value of this experience exchange network grows exponentially as the number of members increases. Although early members accumulate less experience, their learning process itself becomes reference material for later members -- recorded learning paths, difficulties encountered, and solutions found are all valuable knowledge assets.
